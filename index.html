Numbering guide

The pattern underneath the map.

01

Mainline routes

Primary Interstate routes use one or two digits. Odd numbers usually travel north-south, even numbers usually travel east-west, and larger numbers trend north or east.

02

Auxiliary routes

Three-digit routes usually borrow the final two digits from a parent. An even first digit usually means a loop or bypass; an odd first digit usually means a spur or connector.

03

Non-contiguous programs

Hawaii, Alaska, and Puerto Rico have separate federally recognized Interstate programs with local route labels such as H-1, A-1, and PRI-1.
